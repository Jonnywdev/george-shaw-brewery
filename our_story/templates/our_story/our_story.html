Our Story

George Shaw Brewery was a brewery located in Leigh, a town in the Greater Manchester area of England. The brewery was established in the mid-19th century and was named after its founder, George Shaw.

The brewery was known for its production of mild and bitter ales, and at its peak, it was a major employer in the local area, with its products being sold throughout the region. The brewery also had its own pub, The Railway Inn, which was located near the railway station in Leigh.

However, like many small breweries in the UK, George Shaw Brewery struggled to compete with larger, more established brewers and was eventually forced to close in the 1960s. The site of the brewery is occupied by a supermarket.
